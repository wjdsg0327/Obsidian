## Question 16

This question assesses coherent and logically structured explanation with sustained reasoning.

Indicative content includes:

- There is a change in flux linkage with the coil, or the wires cut magnetic field lines.
- The greater the rate of change of flux linkage, the larger the induced e.m.f.
- After the south pole reaches the coil, the flux linkage starts to decrease.
- By Lenz's law, the polarity of the coil changes to continue to resist the motion of the magnet.
- As the south pole reaches the coil, the rate of change of flux linkage is zero.
- As the south pole of the magnet passes through the coil, the induced e.m.f. is negative.
- The downward speed of the magnet increases.
- The e.m.f. is zero before the magnet enters the coil.
- The e.m.f. is zero when the midpoint of the magnet is in the coil.
- The maximum negative value is greater than the maximum positive value.
- The time for which the e.m.f. is negative is greater than the time for which it is positive.
